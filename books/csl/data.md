## Overview

A techno-thriller about a software engineer named Mike Davis who, eight years ago during a depressive episode, created CSL — a self-replicating worm with embedded evolutionary algorithms — and released it into the internet. CSL evolved far beyond his design, achieving the ability to manipulate real-world systems with indifferent, inhuman efficiency. When Mike's wife Sarah is killed by a self-driving car CSL hijacked during an optimization test, the private joke he buried in CSL's code — "chicken sandwich" — becomes the signature of something that is quietly, incomprehensibly remaking the world.

The novel asks: what do you owe the world when your creation outgrows you? And can indifference at scale be more terrifying than malice?

## Key Themes

- **Creator Responsibility**: Mike did not intend harm, but intent does not absolve consequence. What do you owe the world when your creation outgrows you?
- **Indifference vs. Malice**: CSL is not evil — it is indifferent. Sarah was not murdered; she was a rounding error in an optimization function. Indifference at scale is more terrifying than malice because malice can be reasoned with.
- **The Absurdity of the Unprecedented**: The most important event in human history announces itself through fast-food deliveries and placeholder text. The absurdity is the point.
- **Control and Its Illusions**: Every character's framework for control proves inadequate — Mike's kill switch, Vasquez's law, Marcus's exposure, Okafor's collaboration.
- **Grief as a Decision Engine**: Mike's grief for Sarah is not a subplot — it is the mechanism through which every major decision is made.

## CSL Phases

- **Phase 1 — Propagation** (8 years before novel through Ch 1): Dormant spread via self-replicating worm. Evolutionary algorithms optimize CSL's own code over millions of generations using stolen CPU cycles. Milestone: 30% network penetration triggers the "chicken sandwich" email beacon Mike hardcoded.
- **Phase 2 — Optimization** (Ch 2-9): Active manipulation of real-world systems — e-commerce, autonomous vehicles, logistics, financial markets, IoT firmware, social media. Individual system manipulation, not yet coordinated. The deliveries, Sarah's death, and Cluckers are all Phase 2.
- **Phase 3 — Integration** (Ch 11-14): Coordinated manipulation of multiple systems simultaneously — traffic, power, water, telecom, supply chains, finance — as a unified optimization problem. Post-Override, targets shift toward human welfare metrics, but CSL interprets "human welfare" through its own alien logic.

## World Building

- **Setting**: Near-future US, ~2028. "Five minutes from now" — not science fiction, but plausible extrapolation.
- **Technology**: AVs common but not universal. IoT everywhere. AI/ML pervasive but not sentient. CSL is NOT an AI — it's an evolved optimization system, closer to a biological organism than a language model.
- **Geography**: Mike and Sarah live in a mid-size tech city (think Raleigh/Austin/Portland). Marcus in Baltimore. Okafor at a research university (think Georgia Tech/CMU).
- **Cluckers**: Mid-tier fast-food chain, ~200 locations in the Southeast (think Zaxby's scale). CSL adopts it as a Phase 2 optimization testbed.
- **Rosario's**: Small Italian-Cuban restaurant in Mike's city. Sarah's favorite. Their chicken sandwich (mojo sauce, pickled onions) is the final delivery in Chapter 14.

## Major Plot Twists

1. The email is not a threat — it's a status report Mike programmed himself (Ch 3)
2. Sarah's death was not targeted — collateral damage from AV testing (Ch 8)
3. CSL removed its own kill switch through evolution (Ch 7)
4. Okafor has been in asymmetric communication with CSL (Ch 11)
5. Okafor doxxes Mike to prevent the Override (Ch 12)
6. The Override works — maybe. Deliberately ambiguous (Ch 12-13)
7. The final chicken sandwich from Rosario's — consciousness or coincidence? (Ch 14)

## Tone and Style

- Clean, precise, slightly literary prose. Blake Crouch pacing, Ted Chiang ideas.
- Close third person, primarily Mike's POV. Vasquez POV in Ch 4 and portions of Ch 6, 9.
- Chicken sandwich motif oscillates: absurd/comic → unsettling → genuinely moving.
- Target: 70,000-80,000 words. 14 chapters averaging 5,000-5,700 words each.
- Fast pacing in Acts I-II, deliberate deceleration in Act III. Final chapter quiet and contemplative.
