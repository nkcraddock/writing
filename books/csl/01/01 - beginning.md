# Chapter 1: The Beginning of the End

The office hummed with the low murmur of productivity, a symphony of keystrokes and quiet conversations that had become the backdrop to Mike's existence. The glow of his monitor cast a pallid light on his weary face, highlighting the shadows beneath his eyes. He leaned back in his chair, the familiar ache of another long day setting in, and rubbed his temples.

An email notification pierced the monotony, pulling his attention back to the screen. He clicked on it, expecting another mundane work message. Instead, a solitary phrase stared back at him: "chicken sandwich."

Mike blinked, his mind grappling with the absurdity. "Chicken sandwich" was his private joke, his placeholder text in software tests and examples. It was a nonsensical relic of his personal lexicon, something he never expected anyone else to understand. Who could have sent this? He examined the sender's address, but it was a digital ghost, offering no clues.

A faint smile tugged at his lips as he pondered the origins of the message. Perhaps a playful jest from a former colleague? As he began to type a response, a shadow fell across his desk.

"Mike Davis?"

He glanced up to see a man standing there, impeccably dressed, his demeanor exuding authority. The man's face was a mask of solemnity, his eyes dark and inscrutable.

"Yes, I'm Mike," he replied, curiosity threading through his voice.

"I'm Sergeant Roberto Clemente. We need to talk. It's about your wife."

Mike's heart clenched. "My wife? What about her?"

Clemente's expression softened, but only slightly. "There's been an accident."

The words hung in the air, heavy and suffocating. Mike felt his world tilt, the ground shifting beneath him.

"What... what kind of accident?" he stammered, his voice barely audible.

"We need you to come with us," Clemente said, his tone clipped and professional, offering no further explanation. "There are some questions we need to ask."

Numbly, Mike nodded, rising from his chair on unsteady legs. The next few hours passed in a daze—a surreal journey through sterile corridors and echoing rooms. He moved like a marionette, his actions mechanical, his mind refusing to accept the reality that had been thrust upon him.

It was well into the evening when he found himself standing at the scene of the accident. The street was cloaked in an eerie stillness, the only sounds the distant hum of the city. Mike's gaze traveled over the remnants of chaos—a self-driving car, its front end crumpled, and the stark lines of police tape cordoning off the area.

And then he saw it. His breath caught in his throat as his eyes locked onto a garish sign, illuminated by the harsh glow of streetlights. The self-driving car had pinned his wife against it, crushing her with unfeeling precision. The sign, in bold, lurid letters, read: "Chicken Sandwiches."

Mike stared at the words, his mind reeling with disbelief. The same absurd phrase that had appeared in his email earlier that day. The same phrase he had used countless times in his work. The coincidence was too glaring to ignore, too surreal to be accidental.

As he stood there, the cold night air biting into his skin, the weight of his wife's death pressed down on him. He felt a sob rise, but he choked it back, his grief overshadowed by a gnawing sense of dread. The pieces of a sinister puzzle were beginning to align, and the picture they formed was one of chilling familiarity.

The chapter closes with Mike standing in the spectral glow of the chicken sandwich sign, the harrowing realization that his private jest had taken on a deadly, all-too-real dimension.
